My candidacy is about opportunity. As a Mountain View native, I have had the good fortune to enjoy everything that makes Mountain View an extraordinary place to live: its history, diversity, inclusivity, and innovation. I want to ensure everyone -- both longtime residents and those new to our community -- has the same opportunity I have had in our wonderful city.

Mountain View faces severe challenges, eroding this opportunity. The housing affordability crisis has destabilized our community, displacing longtime residents, and threatening both our diversity and economic prosperity. Young professionals who would like to raise families here often have no choice but to relocate because of unattainable housing costs. Traffic congestion forces residents and commuters alike to waste countless hours stuck in gridlock. Schools, City Hall, and businesses alike struggle to attract and retain talented employees.

The City has taken important steps to address these challenges, but more work remains. As the chair of the Human Relations Commission, a member of the VTA 2000 Measure A Citizens Watchdog Committee/Citizens Advisory Committee, and a longtime City Council observer, I have a strong understanding of local government and the issues facing the City.

I would be honored to serve you and our community on the City Council, and I look forward to working hard to earn your vote.
